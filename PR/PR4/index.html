    A.L.A.R.A- As low as reasonably achievable

Bitewing (BWX)- A radiograph showing the crown of the tooth, specifically intended to 
examine interproximal areas for caries

Computed Tomography (CT)- X-ray images taken from different angles and uses computer 
processing to create cross-sectional images, or slices, of the bones, blood vessels and soft 
tissues inside body

Digital imaging- Dental imaging with digital X-ray sensors provides high-definition, three-
dimensional digital images with 90 percent less radiation exposure than conventional film 
radiography for diagnosing a wide array of conditions

Focal trough- In panoramic radiography, the horseshoe shaped zone of sharpness where an 
object would be imaged in acceptable detail

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)- A technique that uses a magnetic field and radio waves to 
create detailed images of the organs and tissues within your body
