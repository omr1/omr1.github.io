    A typical office visit 
	A typical office visit begins when your general dentist determines that
	supplementary imaging is necessary to diagnose and/or treat oral maxillofacial 
	conditions, the dentist will refer you to a specialist (OMR), who will have more 
	insight and input in reading radiographic images. OMRs office should have 
	access to more complex diagnostic tools and can better diagnose your 
	disorder/condition and suggest a treatment plan to your general dentist, such 
	as: surgical planning for wisdom teeth, tooth extraction, facial pain, oral cancer 
	diagnosis/treatment planning, evaluating tumors, cysts, developmental 
	irregularities in the maxillofacial region, planning orthodontic treatment, 
	assessing endodontic injuries, among others. 

	Oral Maxillofacial Radiology offices uses innovative imaging technology, such as 
	digital imaging, which is faster and easier than conventional X-rays, plain and 
	computed tomography (CT), magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and ultrasound.
