    A referral to an Oral Maxillofacial Radiologist may be essential for any of the following reasons:

    
*Surgical planning for impacted wisdom teeth, tooth extraction, and any other oral surgery procedure that may require sedation or anesthesia

*TMJ related abnormalities or disorders 

*Oral cancer diagnosis and treatment planning

*Evaluation of cysts and trauma in the maxillofacial region

    Some examples of these conditions are as follows:

    Ameloblastoma- Is a benign odontogenic tumor

    	Arises from the enamel organ or dental lamina

	X-ray displays a radiolucent lesion

	More common in the posterior mandibular ramus

	Treatment: Tumor removal or radiation

    Odontogenic Myxoma- Is a benign mesenchymal tumor

    	Arises from the dental pulp origin

	Found almost exclusively in the jaw

	X-ray shows a radiolucent “soap bubble” pattern lesion

	Treatment: Tumor removal or radiation

    Eruption Cyst- Is associated with an erupting primary or permanent tooth

    	It forms on the gingival mucosa above the crown of the tooth

	Mostly seen in children

	The lesion is a soft translucent or bluish swelling

	Treatment: excision of the roof or spontaneous rupture of the roof

    Lateral Periodontal Cyst-Arises from post-epithelial dental lamina

    	Located in the mandible and between the roots of erupted premolars

	Radiographically presents a round or oval uniform radiolucency with well defined borders

	Most common in middle aged men 

	Treatment: enucleation or curettage

    Condylar Hyperplasia – a temporomandibular joint disorder

    	Failure of condylar head to achieve normal size because of congenital and developmental
	abnormalities or disease that affect condylar growth

	Seen in children and adults

	X-ray shows a shortened condylar neck and process 
	
	Treatment: orthognathic surgery, bone graphs, or orthodontic therapy
